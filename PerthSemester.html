My study abroad program in Perth, Australia

My fantastic semester down under.

Posted on September 24, 2018

In the spring of 2018, I studied abroad at the University of Western Australia in Perth. This was my first time in Australia, and the second time I spent an expended period time international since coming to college. While in Perth, I stayed at St. Thomas Moore College, which is the dorm that Notre Dame collaborates with. I took five classes at the University of Western Australia. Those Classes were Data Structures, Process Instrumentation and Control, Intorduction to Ethics, the Philosophy of Religion, and Semiconductors Nanoelectronics. There was a sizeable project for data stuructures, which I have discussed in a different post.

Trip to New Zealand

During my time in Australia, I made two major trips. The first of these trips was to New Zealand during my fall break. My father met me in New Zealand and we spent the week traveling around the north island. Some of the major highlights include a visit to Hobbiton, hiking several mountains, going para-sailing, and visiting a Maori village. New Zealand is a beautiful country, with amazing scenery and friendly people. There was something new around every corner. At one point, my father and I watched a speed sheering compititions, where locals and international farmers raced to see who could sheer a sheep the fastest. I hope to make it back again some day.

Trip to Bali

The second major trip I took was to Bali, in Indonesia. This was a trip I took with my Notre Dame classmates during one of the last weeks of the semester, after the world had died down. My classmates and I spent the week in Seminyak, on the southern part of the island. Bali is interesting in that it is the only Hindu island of Indonesia, a country that is majority Muslim. During my time in Bali, my favorite day was the third day we were there. On this day, we woke up at 3:00 am and took a cab to a mountain near the center of the island. We then hiked a mountain and watched the sunrise from the top of the mountain, which was a fantastic view (shown below). After that, we hiked down the mountain and went to a natural hotspring spa to relax after the hike. At this point it was lunchtime, so our tourguides took us to a local resteraunt with an all-you-can eat buffet. From there, we headed to a coffee plantation and learned about the coffee industry in Bali. We finished the day off by eating dinner and listening to live music in our hotel. It was one of my favorite days of the semester, and possible of my college experience.
