Master's in Business Administration Module 1

Starting my journey into the business world.

Posted by Sean Scannell on September 24, 2018

The Decision

During the middle of my junior year, I realized that I was not passionate about engineering. I enjoyed the challenge and the people that were in my major, but I was finding no joy in the classes. I then heard about a five year dual degree program that Notre Dame offers. With this program, I would graduate in 2020 with both a bachelors in Electrical Engineering and a Masters Business Administration. I researched more into this possibilty to find out what this program entailed. In order to stay a fifth year and pay another year of tuition, I must be sure that this was the path I wanted. I meet with my advisors and after some deliberation, I decided to apply. I started studying for the required GMAT and filled out the application. I took the GMAT in the first couple months of 2018 and scored a 720. This boosted my application and I was accepted.

The Start

The University of Notre Dame's MBA class of 2020.

For the fall of 2018, I am taking Financial Accounting, Corporate Finance, Foundations of Business Ethics, Career Leadership, and Business Statistics. These are going well and have been an interesting exposure to the business world. This has been extremely beneficial to me as I am exposed to a wide variety stories from all of my classmates. There are veterans, consultants, sales personal, engineers, etc. The class has been divided into learning teams comprised of 5 members. These teams work together on homework assignments and form study groups for the semester to build a team based mentality in the MBA class, an idea I agree with whole-heartedly.

The module ended on October 4th with final exams. At the end of this module, I averaged approximately a 3.33 GPA. After some reflection, I find this unsatisfactory and will strive more in the second half of the semester to raise this.

Module 2

For module 2, I will be taking Marketing Management, Strategic Decision Making, Financial Accounting 2, and Managerial Economics. The only continuation from last module is accoutning, which is beign taught by the same professor. The rest are new topics and I am exictied to get into these topics.

When it comes to expectations, I am have several. In terms of academics, I hope to get above a 3.7 GPA this semester. With this in mind, I will put more hours into studyign throughout the semester instead of focusing in during the final week before exams. I also hope to get involved more with the consulting club to get a foothold in the consulting field. I also plan on renewing my envolvement in SIBC for the coming academic year, but I have yet to discern in what facet.
