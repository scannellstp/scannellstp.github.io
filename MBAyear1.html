Getting started on my MBA

Posted by Start Bootstrap on August 24, 2018

Paragraph 1

The Final Frontier

During the middle of my junior year, I realized that I was not passionate about engineering. I enjoyed the challenge and the people that were in my major, but I was finding no joy in the classes. I then heard about a five year dual degree program that Notre Dame offers. With this program, I would graduate in 2020 with both a bachelors in Electrical Engineering and a Masters Business Administration. I researched more into this possibilty to find out what this program entailed. In order to stay a fifth year and pay another year of tuition, I must be sure that this was the path I wanted. I meet with my advisors and after some deliberation, I decided to apply. I started studying for the required GMAT and filled out the application. I took the GMAT in the first couple months of 2018 and scored a 720. This boosted my application and I was accepted.

The University of Notre Dame's MBA class of 2020.

For the fall of 2018, I am taking Financial Accounting, Corporate Finance, Foundations of Business Ethics, Career Leadership, and Business Statistics. These are going well and have been an interesting exposure to the business world.
